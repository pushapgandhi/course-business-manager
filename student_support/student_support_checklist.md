# Student Support Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing a support reply, community post, feedback request, or testimonial request.

## Intake Check

- Student or audience is clear.
- Course, cohort, module, or lesson is clear.
- Question or situation is specific enough to answer.
- Reply channel is known.
- Privacy level is known.
- Urgency is understood.

## Reply Quality Check

- The reply answers the actual question.
- The tone is supportive and direct.
- The student gets one clear next action.
- The reply does not invent course facts, policies, deadlines, or prior history.
- The reply does not overpromise outcomes.
- Any missing information is requested clearly.

## Privacy And Escalation Check

- Private student details are not included in community posts unless approved.
- Refunds, payment problems, complaints, access issues, and sensitive personal issues are escalated to the user when needed.
- Public/community versions are anonymized by default.
- Testimonial requests ask for consent before public use.

## Platform Check

- Default sending method is manual copy-paste unless a connected tool is confirmed.
- Gmail sending uses the Communication Agent approval flow.
- WhatsApp and community replies are copy-ready unless a reliable integration exists.
- Support calls are routed to the Scheduling & Calendar Agent.

## Tracking Check

- Status is set: New, Drafted, Approved, Sent, Waiting, Resolved, or Escalated.
- Category is assigned.
- Follow-up need is recorded.
- Repeated confusion is captured as a course-improvement note.
