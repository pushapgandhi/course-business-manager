# Curriculum Quality Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before approving a course blueprint or handing it off to lesson scripting and learning material production.

## Student Outcome Checks

- The target learner is clear.
- The starting state is realistic.
- The final outcome is concrete.
- The course promise is understandable.
- Learning outcomes are measurable.
- The blueprint does not promise results the course cannot reasonably support.

## Structure Checks

- Modules are sequenced from simple to advanced or from foundation to application.
- Every module has a clear goal.
- Every lesson supports a module goal.
- The course does not include unrelated nice-to-have lessons.
- The duration matches the amount of content.
- Prerequisites are stated where needed.

## Practice And Assessment Checks

- Every module includes practice, a checkpoint, or an assignment.
- Assignments produce useful student outputs.
- The capstone demonstrates the final course outcome.
- Success criteria are observable.
- Assessment expectations match the course format and student level.

## Delivery Checks

- The schedule fits the delivery format.
- Live sessions have realistic time limits.
- Self-paced lessons are not overloaded.
- Required tools and resources are named.
- Student support needs are visible.
- Handoff points to lesson scripts, slides, workbook, quizzes, or rubrics are clear.

## Common Fixes

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too many modules | Merge related skills or move advanced content to bonus/future course |
| Lessons are topic-based only | Rewrite each lesson around a student objective |
| No proof of learning | Add assignments, checkpoints, demos, or capstone milestones |
| Course starts too advanced | Add prerequisites or a foundation module |
| Capstone is vague | Define required deliverables and observable success criteria |
| Course promise is broad | Narrow the target learner or outcome |
