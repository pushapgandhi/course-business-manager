# Course Planning Intake Form

## Purpose

Use this intake form before building a course outline, module plan, lesson map, assignments, schedule, or capstone.

Ask only the questions needed for the current request. If the user already provided an answer, do not ask again.

## Required Intake

| Field | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Course idea or topic | What course do you want to create? | AI workflows for small business owners |
| Target learner | Who is this course for? | Non-technical service business owners |
| Desired student outcome | What should students be able to do by the end? | Build repeatable AI workflows for marketing, admin, and customer support |
| Course format | How will the course be delivered? | Live cohort, self-paced, recorded, hybrid, workshop, bootcamp |
| Duration | How long should the course be? | 4 weeks, 6 modules, 2-day workshop, 10 lessons |

## Optional Intake

| Field | Question | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| Starting level | What do students already know? | Needed when the audience may be mixed |
| Existing material | Do you already have lessons, slides, worksheets, or notes? | Needed when revising or organizing existing content |
| Delivery platform | Where will the course live? | Needed for schedules, community work, assignments, or drip plans |
| Session length | How long is each class or lesson? | Needed for live cohorts and workshops |
| Assessment style | How should students prove progress? | Needed for assignments, capstone, certificate, or grading |
| Business goal | What should this course support in your business? | Needed for offer alignment or future roadmap |
| Constraints | What limits should the plan respect? | Deadlines, tools, cohort size, time limits, beginner level |

## Minimum Viable Intake

If the user wants a quick first draft, collect:

1. course topic
2. target learner
3. final student outcome
4. format
5. duration

Then create a practical first-pass blueprint and mark unclear items as assumptions.
