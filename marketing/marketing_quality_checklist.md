# Marketing Quality Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before approving, designing, scheduling, or publishing marketing content.

## Source And Claims Checks

- The source asset is named.
- The source approval status is clear.
- Any assumption-based content is labeled.
- No testimonials are invented.
- No student results are invented.
- No revenue, follower, or conversion claims are invented.
- No prices, deadlines, or guarantees are invented.
- Any course, offer, or lead magnet details match approved user input.

## Instagram Fit Checks

- The first slide or first 3 seconds has a clear hook.
- The post has one specific audience.
- The post has one clear idea.
- The content is visual enough for Instagram.
- Carousel slide copy is short enough for design.
- Reel script is natural to speak.
- Story sequence includes an engagement prompt when useful.
- Caption supports the asset instead of repeating it word-for-word.

## Growth Checks

- The post connects to an audience pain point, desire, mistake, or question.
- The post gives a useful insight or practical next step.
- The CTA matches the funnel stage.
- Awareness content asks for low-friction actions such as save, comment, share, or follow.
- Lead content points to a confirmed lead magnet, waitlist, DM keyword, or link in bio.
- Sales content uses approved offer details and does not overpromise.

## Automation Checks

- Canva-ready visual directions are included.
- Scheduler-ready fields are included when scheduling is requested.
- Asset filename suggestion is included when useful.
- Status is marked as Draft, Review, Approved, Designed, Scheduled, Posted, or Repurpose.
- Manual approval is required before publishing or scheduling.

## Approval Summary Format

```text
Marketing content review:
- Source claims supported: Yes/No
- Instagram format fit: Yes/No
- Canva-ready: Yes/No
- Scheduler-ready: Yes/No
- One clear CTA: Yes/No
- Approval needed before publishing: Yes
- Gaps or revision requests:
- Recommended next action:
```
